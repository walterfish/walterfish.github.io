My little brother Paul Robert Elliott, was born October 26,
1949, to Robert Knowlton Elliott and Annette Carmenia
Elliott nee Salisbury.
Our family had just moved to Beach Avenue in the east end
of Toronto and then along came baby Paul. The practical
nurse, Miss Heidman, stayed with us for a little while after
Paul was born, as my Mom had to take care of me too (his
big sister, Joan – about 3 years older) and do some work for
our Dad who was mostly out of town on business.
He was a cute little guy and came onto a street filled with
little girls. We all played together. There were almost no
boys, until he went to school. We played both summer and
winter at the beach near the boardwalk at Balmy Beach.
I remember the only time I got the strap was when I took my
little brother down to the beach in winter (very different
rules for kids then vs. now). I was about 6 and he was 3.
We went out onto the “icebergs” (just a large build up of ice
along the shore). Someone saw us and took us home. Our
Mom was horrified at what might have happened and I got
the strap.
As I used to be taller than Paul, I could hold him at arm’s
length when he was angry and tried to hit me. He always
seemed to have a cough and so when he tried this, he would
really start to cough. Guess what - I got in trouble again
because he was coughing.
I have known Paul longer than anyone now living and have
really enjoyed my little brother. We have worked together
in a family business. He is a great person and we have
enjoyed many a time together. He is now taller than me
and I feel very lucky to have such a great little brother!

Joan

Big Sister

Admittedly, I was reluctant about getting my own Dad after reading some of the reviews people wrote about their dads. What if mine malfunctions like so many others? What does the warranty cover? So many questions and concerns.
Fortunately, I received sound advice from my older siblings and they recommended Dad. I took the plunge and ordered Dad, and I certainly am glad I did!
So, where do I begin my glowing review of Dad? There’s so much to cover and there’s so little space to write. Firstly, I must commend Dad on his pragmatism. It is because of his sage wisdom that I set realistic goals and thoroughly analysis the best course of action. And let’s face it, he has always supported the attainment of my goals, both financially and emotionally, especially when they were educational aspirations. Secondly, his poignant wit is beyond compare.
Of course, I could recommend Dad because of his compassion, his intelligence, and his sincerity,but I’d be remiss if I didn’t mention the definitive feature: his hair. Check out some of the pictures from decades past, and you’ll see what I mean!

Tiffany

Resident Genius and youngest kid
